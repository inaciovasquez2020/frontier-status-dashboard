What URF is

The Unified Rigidity Framework is a research program that treats combinatorial / graph rigidity, information-theoretic capacity, and formal verification as one object. Its central claim is narrow: a small family of cycle-overlap rank invariants determines when a structure is locally rigid, and the same invariants govern bounds on capacity and dissipation in systems built on top of it. URF ships as a trusted Lean base (urf-core, CorrRank), a reference implementation (chronos-urf-rr), and a set of conditional applications. It is not, and does not claim to be, a resolution of any Millennium-style problem.
